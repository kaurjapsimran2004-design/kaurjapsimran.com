TEXT ME BACK

A playful relationship gifting brand that celebrates the funny, awkward, and honest moments couples experience every day.

CategoryBranding + Packaging
DeliverablesGift Bag, Gift Box, Cards, Certificate, Snack
TypeMajor Studio Project

The Challenge

While shopping for my boyfriend's birthday, I went looking for funny relationship cards that felt modern and relatable — and came up empty. Most of what I found was either too romantic or too generic. I wanted something that reflected the funny and awkward moments real couples actually experience, for an audience of couples and young adults aged 18–35, delivered through humour, conversation, and thoughtful design.

The Approach

Research into Hallmark, Etsy, Lovebox, and Uncommon Goods showed a market full of traditional romance and sentimental keepsakes — but nothing built around everyday relationship humour. I designed around Binny, a 29-year-old in a long-term relationship who loves thoughtful gifts, relationship humour, and saving funny conversations with her partner. The logo grew out of an undelivered text message: the speech bubble represents communication, while the "Not Delivered" label reflects relatable relationship moments, set in an SF-style typeface that feels instantly connected to modern texting culture. Energetic orange paired with warm pink kept the palette playful and emotionally expressive rather than traditionally romantic.

The Outcome

A complete gifting system: a gift bag and box, conversation cards designed to be shared after a fight, a tongue-in-cheek Certificate of Relationship Insurance, and an Emotional Support Snack with nutrition facts rewritten as a fake apology. Every touchpoint carries the same wordmark, colour palette, and "Not Delivered" joke, tying the full package into one consistent, playful brand voice from concept to final execution.
